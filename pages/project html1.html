1. Friendly and Knowledgeable Staff: Staff should greet customers warmly and offer assistance in selecting fruits. They should be approachable and ready to answer any questions. Product Knowledge: Knowledgeable staff can provide advice on fruit ripeness, seasonality, and how to store or prepare different types of fruit. They should be able to recommend alternatives if a particular fruit isn’t available.
        
2. Personalized Service Special Requests: If a customer is looking for something specific, like exotic fruits or organic options, the staff should be helpful and either guide them to those items or suggest an alternative. Recommendations: A helpful staff member might recommend fruits based on the customer’s preferences or offer tips on how to incorporate them into meals or snacks.
        
3. Returns and Complaints Customer Satisfaction: If a customer finds that the fruit they purchased is spoiled or damaged, a good fruit shop should have a flexible return or exchange policy to ensure satisfaction. Problem Resolution: Staff should handle complaints or concerns professionally, offering solutions or compensations when needed.
